About me

I specialize in steady-state designs and dynamic studies of wind farms, centralized and distributed solar stations, and DC/AC coupled and stand-alone Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS). I was involved in the planning and design of more than 1-Gigawatt power plant generation capacity, including the development, estimation, pre-construction, engineering and design, construction, and post-energization phases. I support the development of wind, solar, and BESS projects for numerous clients with varying scopes (e.g., Detailed Design, Owner's Engineering, and Independent Engineering). My experience includes collection system design, North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC) compliance study, interconnection feasibility study, layout development, EPC specification development, technical due diligence, project finance, and solar and wind production estimates. I am also a pioneer in Transmission and Distribution Co-simulation and the Techno-economic Analysis of Battery Energy Storage Systems in Transmission System. I am a Licensed Professional Engineer in the states of California and Texas. I hold a Doctor of Philosophy in Electrical Engineering (Ph.D.) from Colorado School of Mines and a Master of Business Administration (M.B.A.) from the Johns Hopkins University.
